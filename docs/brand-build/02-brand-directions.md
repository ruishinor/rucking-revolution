# 02 Brand Directions

## Decision Summary

Two brand territories are strong enough to carry the full system.

- `Version A - Quiet Momentum`
  - recommended default
  - warmer, more book-friendly, more accessible
- `Version B - Carry Forward`
  - stronger social punch
  - more movement-driven, sharper edge

Both keep discipline. Only one should become the master system.

---

## Version A - Quiet Momentum

### Positioning

Rucking Revolution as the habit-building brand for ordinary adults who want steady, practical progress through rucking.

### Best For

- beginner ebooks
- Medium articles
- evergreen search traffic
- 35+ audiences
- readers who want calm authority

### Brand Line

Start simple. Build strength. Stay consistent.

### Personality

- grounded
- literate
- generous
- practical
- steady

### Voice

Use:

- short claims followed by proof
- encouragement without pep-talk cliches
- practical verbs like start, carry, track, recover, repeat

Avoid:

- mission theater
- pseudo-military commands
- aggressive identity signaling

### Visual Idea

A premium habit-and-progress system:

- warm paper surfaces
- dark pine structure
- clay highlights
- measured progress cues, index labels, checkpoint markers, and simple forward rhythm

### Logo Direction

The strongest territory is now wordmark-led rather than icon-led.

The current best logo cues are:

- a recognisable `Rucking Revolution` wordmark
- a restrained progress signal such as step stacks or waypoint punctuation
- one-color primary execution for most uses

See the isolated refinement file for the locked shortlist.

### Color System

- Canvas: `#F5F0E6`
- Paper: `#FFFDF8`
- Pine: `#2F4B3C`
- Graphite: `#172023`
- Clay: `#BB6A3A`
- Moss: `#6E8B62`
- Field Gold: `#C49A4A`

### Typography

Web:

- Display: `Archivo`
- Reading: `Source Serif 4`
- UI: `IBM Plex Sans`
- Meta: `IBM Plex Mono`

Ebook-safe fallback:

- Display: `Arial Narrow Bold`
- Reading: `Georgia`
- UI: `Arial`
- Meta: `Courier New`

### Website Expression

- Large editorial hero with one clear promise
- Module labels in mono caps
- Product cards with visible preview ratio and one-line value proposition
- Article layouts with pull quotes and progress sidebars

### Ebook Expression

- Cream paper feel
- strong chapter openers
- recurring milestone and reality-check callouts
- simple iconography for posture, gear, duration, and recovery

### Medium Expression

- strong plain-language headline
- short opening paragraph
- one proof block near the top
- subheads every 150-250 words
- one hard CTA and one soft CTA only

### Instagram Expression

- 4:5 carousel-first
- paper card backgrounds with bold pine and clay headings
- checklists, myths, mistakes, and one-screen progress prompts
- use face-light or face-optional imagery; focus on hands, packs, routes, movement

### Strengths

- highest trust
- strongest fit for book sales
- easiest to extend into print and PDF products

### Risk

If handled too softly, it can become overly tasteful and not distinctive enough on social.

## Version B - Carry Forward

### Positioning

Rucking Revolution as a modern civilian movement for everyday endurance and shared progress.

### Best For

- Instagram growth
- community-building
- sharper launch campaigns
- younger urban audience

### Brand Line

Carry forward.

### Personality

- bold
- concise
- energetic
- disciplined
- social

### Voice

Use:

- compact sentences
- strong contrast
- more declarative framing
- identity language around momentum and capability

Avoid:

- macho posturing
- grindset cliches
- fear-driven copy

### Visual Idea

A clean civic-performance system:

- high-contrast surfaces
- bold blocks of signal color
- grid overlays
- directional arrows
- route coordinates and stat chips

### Logo Concept

A stacked pack silhouette and horizon-line monogram, paired with a heavier wordmark.

Why it works:

- stronger at small digital sizes
- more emblematic
- more "movement brand" than "publishing house"

### Color System

- Shell: `#FCFAF6`
- Ink: `#151A22`
- Signal Red: `#D94B33`
- Horizon: `#426A63`
- Rust: `#B86A4D`
- Sand: `#E6C368`
- Mist: `#D9DFE3`

### Typography

Web:

- Display: `Syne`
- Reading: `Literata`
- UI: `Public Sans`
- Meta: `IBM Plex Mono`

Ebook-safe fallback:

- Display: `Arial Black`
- Reading: `Georgia`
- UI: `Arial`
- Meta: `Courier New`

### Website Expression

- stronger section breaks
- larger type ratios
- stat-driven hero
- more contrast between editorial and utility modules

### Ebook Expression

- punchier cover hierarchy
- more dramatic chapter openers
- stronger banner callouts and pull quote treatments

### Medium Expression

- punchier top lines
- stronger hook-led openers
- more social share lines and quotable statements

### Instagram Expression

- quote cards
- progress boards
- "do this today" slides
- faster visual rhythm

### Strengths

- more memorable in feeds
- stronger movement-building energy
- easiest to scale into campaigns and merch later

### Risk

Can drift too close to fitness-brand sameness if not anchored by the reading and publishing system.

---

## Comparison

### If The Goal Is Ebook Sales

Choose `Quiet Momentum`.

### If The Goal Is Social Growth Velocity

Choose `Carry Forward`.

### If The Goal Is A Full Brand For Site + Books + Articles + Social

Choose `Quiet Momentum` as master brand, then borrow select campaign assets from `Carry Forward`.

That is the recommended path.

## Lockup Rules For Either Version

- Always keep the brand name fully spelled on book covers and site headers
- Use the mark alone only for avatar, favicon, spine, and watermark use
- Never place the logo on camouflage or photo-heavy backgrounds without a clean container
- Minimum clear space should equal the height of the `R` in the wordmark
- Keep the mark to one color in most use cases

## Photography And Illustration Rules

- civilians over operators
- sidewalks, parks, suburbs, trails, stairs, morning light, real weather
- ordinary packs and realistic gear
- movement, posture, hands, straps, shoes, and route context
- no staged intimidation
