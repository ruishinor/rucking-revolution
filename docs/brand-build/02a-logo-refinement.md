# 02a Logo Refinement

This file captures the refinement round after feedback on the initial 10-logo exploration.

## What Changed

The first exploration proved two things:

- recognition of the full brand name matters
- the most useful secondary cue is habit-building progress, not a symbolic outdoor icon

The strongest ingredients are therefore:

- a recognisable `Rucking Revolution` wordmark
- a restrained waypoint cue or progress cue
- mostly one-color execution

## Recommended Master Direction Name

Use `Quiet Momentum` as the master direction name.

Why it is stronger than `Route Journal`:

- it says something
- it fits city and trail use
- it supports commuting, habit-building, and long-term consistency
- it aligns with the emotional tone of the brand better than a location-based label

## Logo Color Guidance

The initial multi-color marks were exploratory, not final.

For the actual identity system:

- Primary logo color: `#172023` or `#2F4B3C`
- Reverse logo color: `#FFFDF8`
- Accent color use in logo: rare and optional only
- Clay `#BB6A3A` and Gold `#C49A4A` belong more in layouts, callouts, CTA accents, and product packaging than in the master logo

In short:

The full brand palette was considered for the system as a whole, but the logo itself should be simpler than the wider palette.

## Refined Shortlist

### 1. Waypoint Step Wordmark

Likelihood of long-term fit: `94%`

Why:

- preserves full brand-name recognition
- adds a subtle progress signal
- stays broad enough for books, articles, social, and commuting
- feels designed rather than literal

### 2. Waypoint Wordmark

Likelihood of long-term fit: `91%`

Why:

- strongest pure recognition option
- easiest to use consistently
- strongest fit for publishing and ebooks

### 3. Step Stack Lockup

Likelihood of long-term fit: `84%`

Why:

- clearly signals progress and habit-building
- useful for trackers and product systems
- slightly weaker than the wordmark-led options as the master identity

## Recommendation

Lock the brand direction name as `Quiet Momentum`.

From the logo shortlist, move forward with:

1. `Waypoint Step Wordmark`
2. `Waypoint Wordmark`
3. `Step Stack Lockup`

The first option is the best balance.
