Leadership Principles

Introduction

Effective leadership is the foundation of a successful ruck club. These principles guide leaders in creating positive, impactful experiences for all members while fostering growth and camaraderie.

Core Principles

  • Lead by Example: Demonstrate the commitment, attitude, and effort you expect from others.
  • Serve First: Prioritize the needs and development of your members above personal recognition.
  • Communicate Clearly: Share information openly, listen actively, and provide constructive feedback.
  • Develop Others: Invest time in mentoring and creating opportunities for members to grow.
  • Foster Inclusivity: Create an environment where everyone feels welcome and valued regardless of background or ability.
  • Embrace Adaptability: Adjust plans and approaches based on circumstances while maintaining core objectives.
  • Ensure Safety: Prioritize proper form, injury prevention, and risk management in all activities.
  • Build Community: Create connections and shared experiences that strengthen bonds between members.
  • Maintain Consistency: Provide reliable leadership and steady progress toward established goals.

Practical Checklist

  1. Conduct regular one-on-one check-ins with club members.
  2. Seek feedback after each ruck to improve future events.
  3. Rotate leadership responsibilities to develop emerging leaders.
  4. Organize social events outside of rucks to build stronger relationships.
  5. Recognize and celebrate member achievements and milestones.
  6. Stay informed on best practices for safety and effective group leadership.
  7. Plan progressive challenges that match the group's developing capabilities.
  8. Document lessons learned and share them with the leadership team.
  9. Regularly revisit and reinforce the club's mission and values.
