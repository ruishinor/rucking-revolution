Week 1 / Getting Started

Your first ruck should feel almost too easy.

That may sound underwhelming, but it is the right beginning. The point of your first ruck is not to prove toughness. The point is to make the next ruck more likely. When beginners go too heavy or too hard, they confuse effort with progress.

Start with a backpack that fits, a small amount of weight, and a route you already know. The fastest way to make rucking sustainable is to remove the feeling that you are preparing for an event. You are preparing for a habit.

Reality Check

If you finish the first session thinking, "I could have done more," that is usually a sign you judged the opening load well.

  • Pick a route you already walk comfortably.
  • Carry a load that lets you keep relaxed posture.
  • Stop before technique starts to break down.
  • Write down what felt easy, awkward, or unexpectedly hard.
