Isolated Logo Task

Ten broader logo alternatives from the first exploration round.

These concepts were ranked by long-term fit to the existing brand work: civilian-first tone, website and ebook use, city and trail relevance, thumbnail clarity, and how easily the mark can stretch across Instagram, Medium, covers, and product files. The newer recommendation now lives on the refinement page.

Scoring Note

"Likelihood" is a fit estimate, not a claim of objective correctness. It answers one question: if we kept this first-round direction, how likely is this mark to stay appropriate after twelve months of real use?

1. Route Monogram

92%

Two route lines form an abstract double-R without becoming a map pin, shield, or mountain badge.

Why it fits: broad enough for commuting and trails, memorable at small size, and visually tied to progress rather than terrain.

Shortlist

If we reduce this immediately, the strongest shortlist is: Route Monogram, Commuter Path, and Pack Frame. Of those three, Route Monogram is the best all-around fit and Commuter Path is the best urban-first fit.
